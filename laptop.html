Laptops

How to choose a laptop?

Choosing a new laptop is a lot harder than it should be. Every major brand has multiple product lines with overlapping prices and features, and every description is filled with jargon about processors, types of storage, graphics capabilities, screen resolutions and a laundry list of ports and connections.

These are basical things you need to figure out to choose laptop:

  1. Pick a size
  2. Decide screen resolution
  3. Decide a form factor
  4. Work out your ideal keyboard
  5. Do you want a Touchpad?
  6. Decide connectivity
  7. What performance do you need?
  8. Decide your graphics needs
  9. Battery life
  10. Storage

After thinking about these topics you can realize what type of laptop you need. Nowadays there are some common categories of machines, which can help to narrow your search. Some of these categories are listed below with the examples of recommended by LaunchBox products.

Gaming laptops

There's something quite liberating about getting an awesome gaming laptop. The idea that you can play all the best PC games anywhere you want without sacrificing performance is the dream.

So what should you look for in the best gaming laptop? It really comes down to how far you can stretch your wallet. For the best gaming performance, you want something with at least an RTX 2060 or 2070 graphics card, though you might be able to find a handful of systems with higher-end GTX cards for cheaper. For the display, we recommend an IPS screen at 120Hz and an i7 processor. If you're being more cost conscience, make sure you don't get anything less 16GB of RAM or a 256GB SSD, as those are corners that cheaper laptops tend to cut.

Everything else is really up to you. Jet setters will need a good battery life for travel. If you plan on watching or editing tons of video, OLED screens are absolutely stunning for that. A decent built-in webcam for video calls is often very overlooked on gaming laptops as well. At the end of the day, think about what's important to you and go from there. Either way, this list has got all sorts of killer laptops for whatever you need.

Key features:

  • unusual gaming design
  • great productivity
  • high quality screen and speekers
  • specific keybord with hard buttons
  • extra powerfool cooling system

LaunchBox recommends:

  • Asus Rog Strix

Ultrabooks

The best Ultrabooks are more than just status symbols. These sophisticated and slim laptops are also able to deliver exceptional performance for productivity and to get you through your creative endeavors.

As they’re ever so light and compact, they’re the ideal grab and go machines, which means that you can edit your photos, render your videos and power through your work, no matter if you’re on a train in Europe, at a café, or in between classes.

Even more, they now pack more power than ever before, coming equipped with the best processors and SSDs out there. They truly are powerful little devices that can help you breeze through projects like no one’s business. And, frequently that power is paired with a pretty extraordinary battery life that may last you an entire workday on one full charge.

Key features:

  • thin and light
  • great battery life
  • huge storage capacity
  • processing speed

LaunchBox recommends:

  • Lenovo Yoga 730
